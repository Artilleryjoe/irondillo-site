5 Ways to Stay Safer Online Right Now

You don’t need to be a tech wizard to stay safe online. Just a few simple habits can make you way harder to hack. Let’s hit five of them—fast, clean, and Iron Dillo–approved.

1. Stop Reusing Passwords

Use a password manager like Bitwarden or KeePass. Each site gets its own strong password—no repeats.

2. Turn On Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

App-based codes like Authy or Google Authenticator are better than SMS. Use them anywhere you can.

3. Update Your Stuff

Browsers, phones, apps—if it runs software, patch it. Most hacks come from old junk.

4. Don’t Click Suspicious Links

Emails, texts, DMs—double check the sender, hover before you click, and never trust a link you didn’t ask for.

5. Avoid Running as Admin

Use daily accounts with limited privileges. If malware hits, it can’t hijack your whole system.
