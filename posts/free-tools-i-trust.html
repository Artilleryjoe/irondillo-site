Free Tools I Trust (and Why You Should Too)

Not every cybersecurity tool needs to come with a price tag—or an agenda. These are the ones I actually use, recommend, and trust to get the job done right.

Bitwarden – Password Manager

If you're still reusing the same passwords—or keeping them in a notebook—it's time to level up. Bitwarden is open-source, zero-knowledge, and works across all your devices.

bitwarden.com

uBlock Origin – Browser Protection

This isn’t just an ad blocker—it’s a privacy wall. uBlock Origin filters out trackers, malware scripts, and telemetry so you can browse faster, lighter, and safer.

ublockorigin.com

Have I Been Pwned – Breach Checker

Your email might already be floating around hacker forums. This tool lets you check if your accounts have been exposed in known breaches—and prompts you to act before it’s too late.

haveibeenpwned.com

VirusTotal – File & Link Scanner

Get a second opinion—actually, get 70. VirusTotal checks suspicious links or files against dozens of antivirus engines before you ever click “open.”

virustotal.com

SimpleLogin – Email Aliases

Tired of giving your real email to every site? SimpleLogin lets you create masked aliases—so you can sign up, test things out, or block spam without exposing your inbox.

simplelogin.io

These aren’t flashy tools. They’re reliable, transparent, and genuinely helpful—just like the kind of cybersecurity Iron Dillo stands for.

No sponsorships. No kickbacks. Just solid tools I use myself.
