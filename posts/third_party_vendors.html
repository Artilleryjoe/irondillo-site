Vendor Cybersecurity: Why Trust Isn’t Enough

If your vendors aren't secure, neither are you. Here's how to check before trust becomes a liability.

Your business might have solid cybersecurity practices—but what about your vendors? A weak third-party can be an open backdoor to your systems.

Attackers know companies rarely vet every vendor. They exploit trust relationships—like a cleaning service with Wi-Fi access or a billing provider with login credentials.

Real Risks from Trusted Partners

  • A vendor’s email account gets hacked and sends phishing to your team
  • A software vendor pushes an update laced with malware (e.g., SolarWinds)
  • A service provider stores your data in insecure cloud storage

If your vendor gets breached, you could face downtime, data loss, or worse—regulatory penalties and customer distrust.

How to Reduce Third-Party Risk

You don’t need to ditch your partners. You just need a vetting process:

  • Ask vendors about their security practices and policies
  • Limit access—give vendors the *least* privilege necessary
  • Use contracts that include data protection requirements
  • Review who has remote access to your systems regularly
  • Back up everything before integrating new vendors

Trust is earned—but verify anyway. A little paranoia is healthy when it comes to cybersecurity.
