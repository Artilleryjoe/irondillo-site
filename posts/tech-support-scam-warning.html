5 Ways to Stay Safer Online Right Now

You don’t need to be a tech wizard to stay safe online. Just build smart habits that stick—here are five high-impact moves anyone can start using today.

1. Stop Reusing Passwords

If one site gets breached, and you’ve reused that password elsewhere, your other accounts are now open doors. Use a trusted password manager like Bitwarden or KeePass to generate and store unique passwords for each login.

2. Use Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

2FA is a second lock on your account, and it blocks most brute-force and credential-stuffing attacks. Use an app like Authy or Google Authenticator. Avoid SMS-based 2FA if possible—it’s better than nothing, but less secure than app-based tokens.

3. Update Your Devices Regularly

Patches exist for a reason—most cyberattacks succeed because of old vulnerabilities. Turn on automatic updates for your operating system, browser, and any software you depend on. Set it and forget it.

4. Don’t Trust Every Link

Phishing is still the number one way people get compromised. Be cautious with emails, texts, or messages urging immediate action. Hover over links to verify their destination, and never download files you weren’t expecting—especially ZIPs, PDFs, and EXEs.

5. Stop Using Admin Accounts for Everyday Tasks

Running as administrator all the time gives malware too much power. Use a limited user account for daily activities and only elevate privileges when needed. It’s a small change that limits big damage.

Online safety doesn’t have to be expensive or overwhelming. These five steps offer outsized protection for minimal effort—ideal for small business owners, families, and anyone who’s done being low-hanging fruit for cybercriminals.
