Vendors and Trust in Cybersecurity

If you trust your vendor with your systems, you’re trusting their mistakes, too. Here’s how to protect yourself.

Your vendor is part of your attack surface. If they can log in, install software, or manage systems remotely, their vulnerabilities become your vulnerabilities.

Think of your vendors like roommates. If one leaves the door unlocked, you’re both at risk.

Risks to Watch For

  • Vendors using the same passwords across multiple clients
  • Lack of multi-factor authentication for vendor accounts
  • Remote management tools with open access
  • Unclear or missing contracts about data handling

Even something as simple as an HVAC vendor using remote access (like in the 2013 Target breach) can open you up to disaster.

How to Vet a Vendor

  • Ask if they use MFA. No exceptions.
  • Request their incident response process in writing.
  • Use separate accounts and log everything they access.
  • Include security clauses in your contracts.
  • Get a list of subcontractors. Know who really has access.

Trust… But Verify

You don’t need to be paranoid—you need to be prepared. Your vendors should welcome these conversations. If they don’t, find new ones.

If a vendor refuses to be transparent, that’s your red flag.
